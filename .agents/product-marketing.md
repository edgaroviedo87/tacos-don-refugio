## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Tacos Don Refugio — "El Placer del Sabor": a beloved, decades-old taquería in Villa de Álvarez, Colima, now with a modern digital home.
**What it does (Phase 1):** An informational website that shows the menu (tacos, tortas, órdenes), posts dated announcements ("avisos" — closures, price updates), shows an event/catering gallery, lists the 3 branches with hours and maps, and routes customers to call/WhatsApp. It turns ~10k social followers into an owned audience.
**Product category (the "shelf"):** Local taquería / Mexican restaurant in Villa de Álvarez — how people search: "tacos Villa de Álvarez", "taquería Villa de Álvarez", "tortas Villa de Álvarez", "tacos a domicilio Villa de Álvarez".

**Product type:** Local food business (dine-in, to-go, phone/WhatsApp delivery today). Future: owned online ordering + native mobile app.
**Business model:** Direct food sales across 3 branches; "$$" economical–mid pricing. Strategic goal: shift delivery from marketplaces (Didi/Uber Eats, ~25–30% commission) to owned channels to retain margin + customer data.

## Target Audience
**Geography:** Villa de Álvarez, Colima (~100k+ metro area), word-of-mouth dominant market.
**Primary use case:** Satisfy an "antojo" for authentic, generous tacos — quickly, from a name the city already trusts.
**Jobs to be done:**

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem (customer):** Craving trustworthy, generous tacos and wanting an easy, modern way to find the menu, hours, and to order — without friction.
**Core problem (business):** A decades-old, beloved brand whose relationship with customers is increasingly intermediated by marketplaces that take 25–30% commission and keep the customer data.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Facebook/Instagram = rented land: Meta owns the followers, throttles reach, and isn't a clean menu/hours/ordering home.
- Marketplaces (Didi/Uber Eats) commoditize the brand into a price-sorted list and take the margin + data.
- Phone-only delivery doesn't scale or build an owned, contactable audience.
**What it costs:** lost margin (commissions), no owned customer data/CRM, brand commoditization, missed local-search discovery.
**Emotional tension:** pride in a loved local brand vs. fear of being "rented back" your own customers by the apps; wanting to modernize without losing authenticity.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other local taquerías & street stands in Villa de Álvarez — fall short on brand equity/trust; Don Refugio has decades of reputation and 90% recommendation no newcomer can buy.
**Secondary:** Delivery marketplaces (Didi Food / Uber Eats) — provide demand + logistics but commoditize the brand, take 25–30%, and withhold customer data.
**Indirect:** Cooking at home / other fast food — convenience and price; Don Refugio wins on craving ("antojo"), tradition, and generous portions.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Genuine, decades-old local love & trust ("toda una tradición en Villa de Álvarez").
- Distinct flavor + generous portions ("buen sabor, buenas porciones").
- 3 branches, broad local reach (~10k FB followers).
**How we do it differently:** modernize the *convenience* (clean website, WhatsApp ordering, future owned app) without changing the *soul* (warmth, tradition, family/equipo Don Refugio).
**Why that's better:** customers get the brand they already love, now frictionless to reach; the business keeps margin + the customer relationship.
**Why customers choose us:** tradition & trust, the antojo, local pride & warmth.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Is this still the same Don Refugio I've always loved?" | Yes — same recipes, same team, same tradition; we just made it easier to reach. Modernize convenience, not the soul. |
| "Why order direct instead of the app I already have?" | Better price, no wait, and rewards — "Pide directo y gana." Same food, more for you. |
| "Are the prices on the site current?" | Menu shows a clear "precios sujetos a cambio" note + last-updated date; avisos announce changes ahead of time. |

**Anti-persona:** customers outside the delivery/service area; bargain-only diners with no loyalty; anyone expecting national-chain uniformity over local character.

## Switching Dynamics (toward owned ordering)
**Push:** marketplace fees/commoditization (business); app friction, fees, impersonal experience (customer).
**Pull:** a trusted brand made easy to order from directly; rewards for ordering direct; warmth and recognition.
**Habit:** customers default to the marketplace app already on their phone; business defaults to phone-only delivery.
**Anxiety:** business — owning delivery logistics, operational load on a family team; customer — "is direct ordering reliable?"

## Goals
**Business goal (Phase 1):** turn passive social reach into an owned, contactable audience and become the canonical "where/what/how to order" hub; dominate local search.
**Key conversion actions (Phase 1):** "Pide por WhatsApp" / call; "Cómo llegar" (maps); follow on social; (later) "Club Don Refugio" signup.
**Phased path:** P1 website + audience → P2 online menu + admin → P3 direct online ordering + payments → P4 mobile app + CRM/push.
**North-star (Phase 1):** number of owned contacts captured (WhatsApp list + Club Don Refugio) and Google reviews growth per branch.
**Current metrics:** ~10k FB followers, 90% recommendation; owned audience ≈ 0 (to be built).
